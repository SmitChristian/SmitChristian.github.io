CSCE242 CubeSat Documentation Project

A CubeSat is a small, short time use satellite that is used for small missions that larger, more expensive satellitescannot complete. This webpage will hold the documentation of the project such that future classes can access the information for when they design the CubeSat V3. Documentation should consist of each stage of the project. This being the initial project planning phase, the conceptual design phase, the detailed design phase, construction phase, and the finalization phase. Each section will include documents created and used as well as descriptions of the thought processes. To learn more regarding what a CubeSat is, please view the video below by the Canadian Space Agency.

Project Requirements

  • 1. Design a Low-Cost CubeSat and Ground Station
  • Design and build an earth-orbiting cubesat and appropriate ground station
  • - The cubesat must be able to take and store a photo (min.resolution of 480x480 pixels) of earth every minute and tranmit them to the ground station.
  • - The ground station operators must be able to recieve and display cubesat data including:
  • - Cubesat status data (power and system status)
  • - On-board remote sensing data (the captured images)
  • - The cubesat will be deployed in an orbit of your choosing and must perform the mission for at least 24 hours after deployment

  • 2. Experimentally Valdiate the Design
  • Build a functional prototype of your cubesat and ground station
  • - Using appropriate ground tests, validate the cubesat performance
