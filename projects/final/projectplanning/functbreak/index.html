The functional breakdown structure describes all the tasks that need to be performed in the mission. By breaking down these mission requirements into functions instead of requiring specific components, this allows the freedom of design to fulfil these in the way thatâ€™s most convenient. Power can be provided to the CubeSat in many ways, but all the proposed designs use electricity. The requirements state that the images must be taken of Earth. There are ways other than orientation control that could fulfil this requirement, but having multiple cameras pointing every direction was too expensive and inefficient to consider. Due to the likely orbit of the CubeSat not being geostationary, multiple ground stations around the planet may need to be made to allow the CubeSat to always have somewhere to send data.
